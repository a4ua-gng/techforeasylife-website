Common questions

Important details schools usually ask first.

Short answers for quick review. A tailored proposal can go deeper based on the institution and use case.

What is the ideal student group size?

The strongest guided experience is usually with around 8 to 12 students at a time, especially when you want active observation, discussion and support. Larger groups can still be planned, but the format may need to shift toward a demonstration or staged rotation.

Can a school fit TEL into existing schedules?

Yes. TEL can be shaped around a regular physics period, ATL lab session, science club, enrichment slot or project week depending on what the school already runs.

Is this only about the launch moment?

No. The value comes from the full learning arc: concept connection, preparation, observation, reflection and responsible decision-making—not just the visible result.

How is safety approached?

Safety is built into the learning model through adult supervision, preparation, readiness thinking and clearly guided participation. TEL treats responsible implementation as part of the educational experience.
