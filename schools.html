Questions institutions ask

Useful answers before we talk.

Which classes is this best suited for?+

The main audience is Classes VIII–XII, with the strongest direct curriculum connection to Class XI Physics. Schools can also adapt the experience for an appropriate STEM club or project programme.

Does the school need to create a new subject?+

No. TEL can be positioned as a physics extension, ATL or innovation-lab module, science-club activity, enrichment programme, project week or teacher-supported STEM demonstration.

Who supervises the practical experience?+

The institution appoints a responsible adult who retains permission, judgement and practical control. Students participate only within the school’s approved learning plan.

What does TEL provide?+

The final scope is agreed with each institution. It can include the TEL kit experience, learning guidance, readiness content and implementation planning appropriate to the agreed programme.

Why is there no price on the website?+

Institution requirements, student groups and programme scope can differ. TEL therefore discusses the proposal and commercial scope directly instead of publishing a one-size-fits-all price.

Can individual students order the kit?+

Not yet. Individual availability is coming soon. The current pathway is designed for schools and learning institutions that can provide the appropriate guided environment.

Can documentation or certificates be included?+

Reflection, documentation and certificate options can be discussed as part of the institution’s tailored proposal. They are confirmed only in the agreed programme scope.
