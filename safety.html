One connected pathway

A digital safety layer around every supervised kit experience.

The learner moves forward only after each checkpoint is completed. The institution-appointed adult keeps permission, judgement and practical control throughout.

01 // ACCESS

Scan and identify

A kit QR code opens SafeFlight. The serial number connects the learner to the correct controlled pathway.

02 // LEARN

Watch and understand

Short, age-appropriate learning modules explain roles, boundaries, conditions and the stop-and-ask mindset.

03 // CHECK

Review the setting

The class confirms field approval, spectator boundaries, communication and current weather with the supervising adult.

04 // VERIFY

Score one hundred

A randomized safety quiz requires every answer to be correct. A new selection appears on each attempt.

05 // CONTROL

Enter Mission Control

Successful learners reach a classroom countdown simulation and can download a learning certificate.

06 // REFLECT

Turn observation into STEM

An optional post-session calculator connects measured distance and angle with basic trigonometry.

Know the roles

Understand who learns, who observes and who keeps responsibility.

One team, clear responsibilities.

The institution appoints a supervising adult. Learners follow the agreed pathway, spectators remain behind the marked boundary, and everyone treats “stop” as an immediate instruction. Completing SafeFlight never transfers control away from the adult.

Respect the kit

Use only the assigned materials and the official learning guidance.

Identify—do not improvise.

Check that the serial matches the assigned kit. Follow TEL’s official video and printed guide under adult supervision. Do not substitute materials, change the design or handle anything outside your assigned learner role.

Read the environment

Conditions can change, so readiness is never a one-time decision.

Look, listen and reassess.

The supervising adult confirms the approved location, the class checks visible conditions and the weather panel adds context. If the field, weather or group setup changes, pause and reassess rather than relying on an earlier check.

Communicate clearly

Shared language helps the whole group make responsible decisions.

Pause means pause.

Before the classroom simulation, everyone should know the supervisor’s stop signal and where to direct questions. If anyone is uncertain, notices a change or enters the wrong area, the correct response is to pause and tell the supervising adult.

Review the setting together.

Confirm each item with the designated adult. The live weather panel can raise cautions but cannot issue approval or predict every local change.
